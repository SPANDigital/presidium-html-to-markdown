Consumer Group’s lag metrics¶

ACI Kafka customers can access lag metrics for their eligible consumer group on Mosaic using Grafana or PromQL. ACI Kafka produces metrics to Mosaic, but the ability to setup alerts requires that you own a dedicated Mosaic workspace. Please follow the Can I set up alerts? section on how to set up alerts based on these metrics.

Note

This tool is provided as-is, without a Production SLA. SLA and SLO will be announced in the future. Contact us if you would like to access your metrics.

What are available metrics and labels?¶

Metrics available to consumer groups are enumerated below.

Metric name

Description

kafka_consumergroup_group_lag

The difference between the last produced offset and the last consumed offset for this partition in this topic partition for this consumer group.

kafka_consumergroup_group_lag_seconds

A rough estimate of the lag in seconds. This metric can be misleading if the traffic flow is inconsistent or spiky.

kafka_consumergroup_group_offset

The last consumed offset for this partition in this topic partition for this consumer group.

kafka_consumergroup_group_topic_sum_lag

The sum of differences between the last produced offset and the last consumed offset of all partitions in this topic for this consumer group. Including idle consumer instances.

kafka_consumergroup_group_sum_lag

The sum of the differences between the last produced offset and the last consumed offset of all partitions for this consumer group. Including idle consumer instances.

Labels¶

Common labels¶

  • _ws_ : Mosaic workspace. This is a constant value set to aci-kafka and cannot be changed.

  • _ns_: Mosaic namespace. Either kafka-consumer-lag-prod or kafka-consumer-lag-if1.

  • cluster_name: Kafka cluster name.

Consumer group labels¶

  • consumer_group: The value of group.id

  • client: ACI Kafka client identity. This value is extracted from consumer_group.

  • client_customer_group: ACI Kafka customer group of the consumer group.

Additionally, the metrics that are not summed include the following label:

  • consumer_id: The id of the consumer group member. Usually, this is automatically generated by the group coordinator. If the instance has been idle for 7 days, then consumer_id will be unknown.

Topic labels¶

  • namespace: ACI Kafka namespace of the topic.

  • customer_group: ACI Kafka customer group of the topic.

  • topic_name: ACI Kafka topic name. This is not the fully qualified name.

  • partition: The Kafka partition.

Where can I check my metrics?¶

To see your dashboard, enter your client and environment in the Lag dashboard.

How do I set up alerts?¶

To set up alerting, you will need to:

  1. On-board with ACI Mosaic.

  2. Once you have your own Mosaic workspace, contact the ACI Kafka team and we will share the metrics with your workspace.

  3. Once this is set up, follow Mosaic’s alerting documentation. If you have any other questions please contact the ACI Mosaic team directly on Slack in #help-aci-mosaic.

Note: Lag metrics are only shared for consumer groups that conform with our naming conventions.

Common problems or errors you may face¶

Why my lag doesn’t go down?¶

Possible causes could be:

  • Double check you are filtering out inactive consumer instances from your query/alert by adding consumer_id!="unknown".

  • If this isn’t working check the history for the last few days to see if your consumer group ever reached zero lag (or near zero lag). If not, check the details of large lag for more details on how to fix this.

  • If you are monitoring lag in time, you could be misled if the producer is replaying old records with old the timestamp.

Why my lag is zero?¶

Your lag will be zero when you consume everything in the topic. However, if you believe your lag shouldn’t be zero then check if the producer is still publishing data.
