Contact Us¶

ACI Kafka is an actively developed and supported service. Here are the various ways you can contact us depending on the kind of support you need.

General Support¶

We are active on Stack Overflow using the tag aci-kafka. It’s our preferred way to provide support for questions that can be asked and answered in an open forum—so as to benefit others.

For questions which are not a good fit for Stack Overflow, contact aci-queue-support@group.apple.com.

If you need specific support for a non-critical issue please file a radar against ACI Queue | Kafka Support.

When contacting us please provide as much information as you can, omitting anything confidential. Consider including:

  • Some context on what you’re trying to achieve

  • A brief explanation of the impact (e.g. development blocker, performance degradation, etc)

  • Details of what you did

  • What you expected to happen

  • What actually happened

  • Details of the client library you are using

  • Details of the client configuration you are using - including your customer group information.

  • Detailed logs - ideally at debug level

  • Tell us what you have what you have already tried any theories you have about the nature of the problem.

On-boarding or Expansion¶

To contact us about on-boarding or expansion see our dedicated on-boarding & expansion page.

Announcements¶

If you would like to stay on top of ACI Kafka news, downtime notifications, releases, and other announcements, please add yourself to aci-kafka-announce in Apple Directory.

Slack¶

There are two channels on Apple Slack for ACI Kafka support.

  • help-aci-kafka, for regular questions about ACI Kafka which don’t fit in Stack Overflow.

  • aci-kafka-prod, only for production incident reporting and related questions on ACI Kafka.

Feel free to post questions or support issues there to get live help.

Note: Although we monitor these Slack channels we offer no SLA for responses there. Therefore consider escalating via email, or if it fits the criteria for urgent support page us.

Paging for Urgent Support¶

For urgent production, customer impacting issues and outages you can page us.

ACI Kafka does not use Help Central for receiving customer support requests. Any tickets you raise there will not receive a timely response.

Note

Please be aware that we have a pager rotation which includes colleagues in the London timezone (UTC/GMT), so a page during Cupertino office hours may wake up a member of the team in London. Please be considerate, and if your issue does not require immediate attention please start with some of the other channels like Slack or email.

If you have followed the troubleshooting guide and determined that you need to page us during an incident - it is required to provide the following information so that we can assist you effectively:

  1. The fully qualified Topic(s), or Namespace(s) (e.g. aci.queue.prod.pv-namespace.events-topic) for which you have observed an issue

  2. A brief explanation of the impact (e.g. service outage, data loss, severe performance degradation, etc)

  3. Some metrics or logs that show the observed the change in behavior/issue you are experiencing

  4. What actions you have already taken to remediate the issue (if applicable)

  5. A brief description of how your use case uses Kafka

The paging email for Queue support is BCC:aci-queue@applepie.pagerduty.com.

A few things to note when using this alert:

  1. It will create a new PagerDuty ticket, so please use BCC: for that address, otherwise every email reply will create a new PagerDuty ticket.

  2. It should be used for genuine emergencies only. Normally an issue in a QA environment is handled during business hours only. If your issue can wait for a few hours then consider another channel.

Regarding a company shutdown like the Thanksgiving or Christmas breaks, there are often change moratoriums in place so that everyone can get a much deserved break.

If a group is planning on working during that time and will need additional support, please have a valid business reason for doing so, and make prior arrangements with the SRE team(s) that may be impacted so that we’re in the best position to address any support needs that may arise.

Design & Development (Apple Internal)¶

We created the aci-queue-contrib Apple Directory group as a forum for Apple wide design discussion to happen. You are welcome to join and suggest features, design or PRs.

Office Hours¶

We don’t have ACI Kafka specific office hours. However you can make it to regular ACI-wide office hours to have your questions answered. Check here for more information or watch out for our announcement email to aci-kafka-announce coming soon.
